Orchid: Names, the past, and the future.

Nov 22, 2023

Welcome to TIL!

Today, Today I Learned becomes TIL: The unofficial Micro.blog companion. Related updates have been made, alongside a number of additions throughout and outside of the site. You can check the changelog for a quick update.

This isn’t the first time in which I have introduced this site and the associated parts of the project. However, names are a funny thing; at once so very important and yet almost irrelevant. If it wasn’t for signs and labels and other such markings I would easily forget a fair-sized list of names.

Given that, I have had an increasing feeling of uncertainty when considering the name “Today I Learned”; specifically when said aloud it can be cumbersome and ill-fitting of my overall goal for the project. A resource for helping people use Micro.blog should not be difficult to even name.

As such the site, and connected parts are now simply “TIL”. There’s also a new domain: til.tips. A new banner is also in place, and relevant parts of the site have been edited to reflect this change, along with a further edit of the tag line:

The unofficial Micro.blog companion.

(for context: “and guide” has been removed from the end)

Brevity, as a quality, feels much more aligned to even the purpose of Micro.blog at the basic level; to be swift and effective. And so here we are. Lastly, I’ve updated the off-site parts of TIL with the name, tag line, and links. There is also a redirect in place for the old name, just in case it’s needed.

Compatibility and clarity

I have a made a new, single-purpose page: Contact. It’s just a simple contact form, an alternative way for folks to send me an email about anything to do with TIL at all.

The contact page is hosted on Letterbird, from the rather clever team at Good Enough and an addition I am very happy to have made; the easier it is for people to let provide feedback or ask questions, the better TIL can become.

Over on the About page I updated links for the aforementioned changes, and added two new sections; my philosophy and my sharing policy. I have thought about both of these sections on and off for quite a while. Whilst it is true that these are the first versions and improvements will be made over time, I did not want even the first version to be the result of a half-baked thought. As such I am so very happy to have finally found the right moment to publish these additions, to better represent the exact “about” of TIL.

I have also made edits to the language on the page, further clarifying and maintaining relevant information.

Next, for the Credits page; I have added a few entries in the form of plug-ins, CodePen, and the “Software” section. The library of Micro.blog plug-ins has grown considerably over the past couple of years and now offers a variety of options for enhancing your blog, alongside excellent support from plug-in authors across GitHub, the Help forums, and the timeline.

CodePen, meanwhile, has been an invaluable resource for quickly testing changes to the site’s theme. It is especially useful given my relative ignorance of software development regarding IDEs and the like. The “Software” section is now fully up to date and reflects my much more settled working environment with regard to tools; each item listed plays and increasingly important role in the work and represents the core of what I do here.

The layout of the lower half of the page has also been improved, with clearer sections and relevant separation of different types of items. This has been further improved with the removal of outdated items.

Lastly, for pages, is Funding. I gave this page something of a maintenance update; with improvements to language, and relevant links and information. The previous set of updates to TIL included much more of a focus for this page, specifically with regard to the Buy Me a Coffee page.

Elsewhere, the Buy Me a Coffee page now has a wishlist. When you visit the page you’ll find it by one of two ways; either via the tab below the page description, or the new widget which shows the top two items and is a portal to the rest of the list. I believe this change makes it easier to see exactly how directly your donations can help my work across all of TIL.

The rest of the page, including some posts, were given the relevant updates for the change in name and domain of TIL. There is also a new announcement covering these updates, specifically within the context of the donations page itself.

The past and the future

As I look to further improve the future of TIL, it became apparent that I still needed to consolidate the past. To begin with this has meant updating the old Updates site by turning it into an archive; mostly this serves to point people in the right direction regarding news about Micro.blog, and I am glad to have settled the old site with an appropriate design.

This feels like the right time to remind you of the primary sources for Micro.blog news:

  • Micro.blog News; the official news blog.
  • @News; the accompanying Micro.blog page for the blog.
  • Manton’s blog; more often than not Manton will at least announce the most substantial updates, and will often go into greater detail about them.

You can see more official sources on the Core links page.

Finally, for this initial set of updates I have create a new off-site branch of TIL; a new newsletter: bts.

The aim of publishing this newsletter is as the name suggests; to provide a look behind-the-scenes of making TIL. That includes details about how I work, thoughts on Micro.blog, previews, and updates about what’s happening and how to get involved.

The welcome issue will be published tomorrow, Thursday the 23rd of November. You can follow along by signing up on the Buttondown page, on which I say:

There is no release schedule, no spam, and no other way to get these posts.

This is a look behind the scenes very much at my own pace, with no plans at all to add any sort of schedule or strict planning. The only exception to this will be during times in which people are generous enough to help me with feedback for previews and the like, and even then the conversations will likely continue in an organic manner, rather than by following a schedule.

The reason I am not actively making the issues available as a blog is because I do not want to make another blog; my exact aim for everything I publish in this newsletter is for it all to fit as a newsletter. As with the nature of this new aspect of TIL, even this condition is subject to change; I have no complicated plan, only what you can already see and so we will see what happens!

Orchid?

This is the first set of updates as part of an upgrade programme for TIL, which I have named Orchid. Over the next few months there will be more updates released in the same way, with the overall aim to provide substantial improvements to TIL in a number of ways.

What’s next

The work for these changes truly progressed over the past few months, in the midst of the most important move our family has ever made. It seemed odd to me that I would spend any time working on anything other than move during this time, however, the way in which we planned the whole process meant that we were able to quickly take advantage of our new locale; specifically, the capacity this place holds for people like us to… take a breath. You know?

Already Cornwall has given us, in no small measure, the opportunities needed not only to find a new home but begin living in what we have quickly come to feel; our place. It has been freeing beyond measure for me, and the work reflects that fact. These updates may have been planned and begun elsewhere, however it is here that I have actually shaped them to fit exactly what it is that TIL ought to be. Especially right now.

Between my continuing satisfaction with omg.lol as host of the changelog and the associated consolidation of meta updates on this site, and now the launch of the bts newsletter it’s fair to say that I’ve finally found a balance for publishing meta updates. That resolution feels particularly good, in that I already feel calm relief for what had become a source of undue stress.

The next step for Orchid will be released soon. This means the return of two important parts of TIL: Guides and regular posting. Finally, having spent an inordinate amount of time wrangling with Guides I have been able to settle on how they should work, both in the context of the whole site and as a singular resource. The regular set of posts, as can be seen on the About page, will return gradually over the course of Orchid; this decision came as a result of the time I spent to reconsider these updates and improve the overall plan, and I am glad to say that those decisions have borne their fruit.

Whilst I do not currently have a publishing date for this next step, there is a chance I will share such a thing in the bts newsletter. In the meantime, I hope this set of updates helps only to further improve the quality of the existing resources here, and I can say with confidence that they have made it much easier for me to now progress into full and proper publishing activity.

I can’t wait for the next part. And I am so very happy to finally be back.

Thanks for reading.

omg.lol

TIL is now on omg.lol! I’ve had a lot of fun setting up the profile page, which is in an early preview mode, and getting to grips with the platform and how it can work with and alongside Micro.blog. A number of folks on Micro.blog already know well the excellent work that has been done by Adam Newbold and the staff over there.

The other public use I have made of omg.lol is the “Statuslog” feature, with which I am now posting the TIL Status Updates. It is here that I provide the small, continuous updates to TIL, many of which were previously included in the Release Notes category on the site. The feed of Status Updates is included in the TIL Micro.blog timeline as some of you might have already noticed, and is available for anybody to use via RSS.

As a result of the new Status Updates feed the news posts on this site will instead be more like this very post; longer posts for announcements and related ideas, with reduced focus on every little change. Since the focus of posts here are as resources for using Micro.blog, it feels good to minimise the amount of meta subject-centred writing whilst also keeping it public in a separate space for those who are interested. Finally, my personal blog will no longer be used as a source of news at all; although I may still link to the most significant updates.

I look forward to trying out more of the ways in which Micro.blog and omg.lol can either interoperate or at least work in a complimentary fashion.

I have made several updates to the site itself; this includes and relates to external platforms.

The header and footer have both finally been improved with regard to the layout of the “Following” icons, the addition of a new separator for the end of posts and pages, and overall design when using smaller screens. The central “Join” column of the footer has also been replaced with a “Support” column, in the process of swapping the membership offering for the donations page.

Given the aforementioned change to how updates are published, the post category for announcements regarding changes has been changed from “Release Notes” to “News”. The ICYMI and About pages have been edited to reflect this switch, whilst the latter also now has an Elsewhere section that includes the newly launched omg.lol and Mastodon extensions of TIL. The support section of the About page has also been updated to switch the previous membership program for Buy Me a Coffee.

The final changes to pages have been for the Funding page, which now includes a much improved list of goals; there is greater detail and the information is up-to-date. I have also expanded the last section of the page, “Making a donation”.

I’ve added a new page: Tweets. This archive is the remaining copy of these posts, since I have deleted this account.

Elsewhere, I have enabled ActivityPub integration so that it is now possible to get a feed of posts from the site via Mastodon; this link has taken the place of the Twitter profile, where relevant, on the site.

It has been both a lot of fun and a relief to work on TIL again. The feeling I get from connecting to more of the open web, at the cost of the closed web, is… comfort, I think?

My previous plans for 2.0 have not worked as I originally had intended, and so my approach has now changed. I have already started writing the next update, covering this exact topic, and so I imagine that will be along much sooner than this post was from the last update. Certainly my path forward is much clearer and it is safe to say that I have found the renewed energy to finally get TIL back on track.

Until the next announcement, I’ll see you all on the timeline.

Release: 1.9.8; an apology, tweaks, and fixes

Jun 29, 2022

With this set of maintenance updates I’m continuing to focus on the changes and fixes that put TIL into the best position possible for the current version. However, some elements of this release are also laying the groundwork for 2.0.

The Changelog

  • Introducing posts are now on hiatus until further notice.
  • Favorites guide edited with note regarding outdated information.
  • Lookup posts:

    • Join column edited for clearer language.
  • ICYMI:
    • A new date format at the top of each issue.
    • Fixed issue number discrepancy.
    • Subscribe page edited for accurate information.
  • Membership:
    • Free registration deactivated since it does not include the expected access to membership posts.
    • Join page edited for accurate information.
    • System emails edited for accurate information.

The Details

The Introducing series of posts highlight different members of the community, including a screenshot of their site and relevant links. When creating and publishing these posts I have never once stopped to ask consent from the people featured therein.

I would like to publicly apologise for this behaviour. It was rude beyond measure; inconsiderate, assuming, and overall reflective of an entitled and reckless mindset. Every person featured in these posts has the opportunity to have their post removed.

To those who I have been unable to contact via email: I am deeply sorry both for having done this and failing to find a way to contact you personally.

I am now correcting this behaviour by including as much as possible the habit of asking consent before taking such actions. I will also do a better job of highlighting the means by which any person referenced and/or linked on the site can have said information removed.

From now on the Introducing post type is no longer a part of the weekly publishing schedule. I hope to still publish it in the future but for now will take the time to work out how best to do so.

The emoji prefixes that were previously part of regular post types (until 1.9.7) are still being gradually removed as I make further progress towards consistent and reliable publishing. The reason I reverted this change (having introduced the emoji in 1.9) is primarily for accessibility; I have discovered that emoji do not work particularly well with website readers and even if they did I realised that this would be a bad experience. The information on this site should be a bridge to making it easier to use and be a part of Micro.blog, and so anything that arbitrarily interferes with the flow of said information will inevitably make it less useful.

As with Introducing, I have learned that I should be much more mindful about such choices and am also working to make sure that the changes for 2.0 are as available as possible to as many people as possible.

Pronunciation details in Lookup posts have been removed as of now, with existing posts to be edited over time. I am not currently able to write phonetic spellings and so should never have included that section of the posts in the first place.

The last version of ICYMI did not have an issue number or any contextual information. I’m sorry for the mistake and am working on getting better at that.

Thanks for reading.

Nov 1, 2019

Following the feature-packed previous month there was a mix of varied, mostly smaller updates in October, which then ended with a couple of big releases.

Let’s take a look at what’s been happening lately on Micro.blog.

In the past month…

Domain Registration

You can now register a custom domain in your account settings. This registration makes it easy for you to get the domain you want and immediately use it with one of your hosted sites; all of the settings are taken care of and the feature includes email forwarding.

To see for yourself: go to the web app -> Account tab -> Paid microblogs section -> Get a Domain Name button. There is also a new document in Help covering the feature.

  • Register your domain name on Micro.blog ‚Ä¢ Help document ‚Ä¢ Domain registration page

Check-in Support

It is now possible for third-party developers to provide check-in posting, after the API was updated to support location data. This includes venue name, URL, latitude, and longitude, and is not currently supported in the official Micro.blog apps.

You can present this data on your site via custom theming, using a parameter for each data type with the exception of venue name, which is automatically included. Manton’s post includes an example of the code you would use in the custom theme.

  • Swarm check-ins and Micro.blog

Photoblogging, Disqus Tutorial, and more!

A few improvements and fixes came to Sunlit throughout the month, including a new option for clearing cahed photos and old drafts. Elsewhere for the photoblogging side of things, the Photos index page was updated to only include JPEGs so that screenshots are much less likely to appear in the grid, and a there’s a new Help document about the page and how to customise it.

Further support for hosted blogs was provided in the form of a new tutorial in Help, with which you can see how to add Disqus comments to your site, and it is now possible to control pagination in settings specifically when using the Marfa theme.

Elsewhere, the iOS app was improved with further Dark Mode support and a number of fixes. Finally, Inktober was again featured in Discover and a new special pin was launched for the month-long event.

After the extraordinary release schedule of September, on the back of a remarkably productive summer no less, this month was inevitably going to have a greater focus on stability and performance maintenance. Also, the previously promised improvements of hosted accounts and the web app have come to pass, whilst there is still seemingly more to come along those lines before the end of the year.

Categories Upgrade

It is now possible to assign a category to a micro post. This is done via the new button on the New Post screen, with which you can control whether to add a category or save the post as a draft.

There is also a new way to manage categories: manual filters. With this feature you can automatically assign posts to categories based on the content of the post title and/or post body. It is also possible to use this feature retroactively, using the Run Filter button to filter existing posts into categories.

Category management was further improved in settings with the addition of number of posts per category. These numbers are also links to filtered lists of posts per category.

  • Filter posts into categories ‚Ä¢ Easier category management

Free Testing

All hosted accounts now have access to a free second blog, should you wish to use it. The intended use for these blogs is for testing anything you might want to try before doing so on your main blog, whether that’s a post, page, file upload, or theme edits; it is the latter for which this new feature is especially well made.

You can now make changes without a care for whether it will have an effect on your main blog, whilst implementing changes are even easier thanks to the new Duplicate button. With this button you instantly make a copy of the theme, which is then available for use on your other blog. This works both ways, since you can then test changes on the test site with the copied theme and when you’re ready to apply those changes to your main blog you can access it since custom themes are shared across your blogs.

  • Micro.blog test blogs ‚Ä¢ Custom themes Help document

iOS 1.7

When signing into an existing account make sure to choose the same address associated with that account.

Let's talk about: Favorites

Feb 12, 2019

Let’s talk about Favorites.

On Micro.blog you can save posts into a separate collection, made available in a special version of the timeline called Favorites. This collection is entirely private and the authors of posts you choose to favorite are not made aware of this action at all; there are no notifications of any kind when it comes to favorites.

Let’s save and view some Favourites.

Whether on the web or in the app, saving favorites means using your main timeline. Make sure you have it loaded.

When you see a post you would like to favorite, look for the Favorite action which can be found in the meta data below the post, next to the Reply action:

Once you have selected the action it changes to Unfavorite:

This means you have favorited that post and it is now saved to your Favorites collection. To view this collection look to the top of your timeline on the web:

… or in the tab menu on iOS:

In there you will see the posts you have saved, as part of your Favorites collection, a static timeline:

One important issue with Favorites is the availability of the feature; whilst it is available via the method displayed above, it is not as consistently available in every view, for example you can favorite replies in certain situations but not all of the time.

You can also use Favorites outside of the official apps, whether in dedicated third-party apps or those that use protocols built on open web standards. For example, you could use the Bookmark feature with OmniBear, Quill, or Indigenous to save anything from across the web.

Favorites can be used in different ways depending on how you’d like to use them;

  • they can serve as a simple bookmark system for different posts and replies, all saved conveniently in a separate timeline;
  • you can also treat them as a read-later timeline for those posts you don’t currently have time to read;
  • lastly, they can be used for grouping together mini-collections by favoriting the individual posts and replies one after the other.

You can use them in one of these ways or a mix of them, such is the flexibility inherent to this simple feature.

As with the rest of Micro.blog, there is no doubt Favorites will be improved over time in a stable, iterative manner; in the meantime you can ask for help and provide feedback via the official support channels.

There we have it; that’s Favorites. Now you know what to do and how to do it.

On Micro.blog you can collect pins for your account, represented by unique icons you can see within your account. They are available on the web and are unlocked by reaching certain milestones or other conditions, such as publishing your first post to the Micro.blog timeline, or posting after midnight.

With that in mind…

Let’s get our hands on the Pins.

Getting a look at the regular pins means using the web. Make sure you’re logged in and ready to go.

Make your way to micro.blog/account/pins – as well as the link at the bottom of the Guide, you can get there by selecting the Account tab:

… and then selecting Show All. Once you have done this you will see the pins screen:

Here you can see all of the regular pins and how you can get them, along with your instantly collected first pin for registering a username! By fulfilling the conditions set out here you will collect the pins; like this:

(NOTE: To collect the Custom Domain pin you will need a hosted account, with which you can map your custom domain to your hosted site.)

The collection of default pins are as follows:

  • Welcome: Every account starts with this pin. It’s a word of thanks for creating an account.
  • First!: You unlock this pin the first time you post to your account.
  • 10 Posts: Once you have published 10 posts you will unlock this pin.
  • 25 Posts: Once you have published 25 posts you will unlock this pin.
  • Daily Blogger: Once you have published at least 1 post every day for 30 days you will unlock this pin.
  • Micro Monday: The moment you partake in Micro Monday for the first time you will have also unlocked this pin.
  • Photoblog: The first time you publish a post with a photo in it you will unlock this pin.
  • Photo Challenge: If you publish a post with a photo every day for 7 days you will unlock this pin.
  • 30-day Photo Challenge: If you publish a post with a photo every day for 30 days you will unlock this pin.
  • Custom Domain: Should you choose to subscribe to a Hosted plan and configure your site with a custom domain this pin will appear.
  • Night Owl: As soon as you publish a post after midnight you will unlock this pin.
  • Bookshelves: Upon adding a book to a bookshelf you’ll unlock this pin.
